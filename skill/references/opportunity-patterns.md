# Opportunity Patterns — From Notes to Output

Use this when running Phase 3 (Opportunity Intelligence) of Vault Intelligence analysis.

---

## Pattern Recognition: Notes → Articles

### Pattern 1: The Dense Cluster
**Signal**: 5+ notes on one topic, many interconnected
**Opportunity**: Long-form article or definitive guide
**Angle**: "Everything I know about X, connected into a framework"
**Example**: 8 notes on AI agents → "A practical map of AI agent architectures"

### Pattern 2: The Surprising Connection
**Signal**: Two separate clusters that share a non-obvious link
**Opportunity**: "The X you didn't know was related to Y" piece
**Angle**: Insight that combines things others keep separate
**Example**: Notes on Zettelkasten + notes on AI training data → "Your note-taking system is secretly a training dataset"

### Pattern 3: The Contradiction
**Signal**: Two notes that disagree with each other (especially across time)
**Opportunity**: "I was wrong about X, here's what I learned" article
**Angle**: Intellectual honesty + evolution of thinking
**Example**: Early note "prompting is just tricks" vs later "prompting is a cognitive interface"

### Pattern 4: The Unanswered Question
**Signal**: A question appears in notes but was never resolved
**Opportunity**: Research piece or open exploration essay
**Angle**: "I'm trying to figure out X — here's what I've found so far"
**Example**: Recurring question about "how do you evaluate agent reliability?" → research article

### Pattern 5: The Hard-Won Lesson
**Signal**: Notes from a project or experiment, with mistakes documented
**Opportunity**: Case study or lessons-learned post
**Angle**: Credibility through specificity and vulnerability
**Example**: Notes from building a RAG pipeline → "What I broke building my first RAG system"

## Pattern Recognition: Notes → Products

### Pattern 1: The Repeated Pain
**Signal**: Same frustration appears in 3+ different notes
**Opportunity**: Tool that eliminates that pain
**Example**: Multiple notes complaining about "summarizing Slack threads manually" → Slack summarizer agent

### Pattern 2: The Manual Workflow
**Signal**: A multi-step process described in detail
**Opportunity**: Automate that workflow
**Example**: Detailed notes on "how I research a topic" → research agent

### Pattern 3: The Expertise Gap
**Signal**: You've gone deep on X but the market teaches X badly
**Opportunity**: Course, guide, or tool for people learning X
**Example**: Deep notes on prompt engineering for non-coders → course for marketers

### Pattern 4: The Unique Combination
**Signal**: Your two clusters together are rare in the market
**Opportunity**: Niche product that serves the intersection
**Example**: AI knowledge + healthcare domain → medical note-taking AI tool

### Pattern 5: The Framework
**Signal**: You've developed a personal system or taxonomy others don't have
**Opportunity**: Template, workbook, or tool built on your framework
**Example**: Your personal "AI tool evaluation criteria" → public scorecard tool

---

## Pattern Recognition: Notes → Automations

### Pattern 1: Repeated Copy-Paste
**Signal**: Notes describe doing the same operation in multiple tools
**Example**: "I take each meeting summary and put it in Notion" → auto-sync automation

### Pattern 2: Information Aggregation
**Signal**: Notes describe gathering data from multiple places manually
**Example**: "I check 5 AI newsletters every morning" → AI digest agent

### Pattern 3: Content Repurposing
**Signal**: Notes describe transforming content formats (blog → thread, transcript → summary)
**Example**: Notes on "turning articles into tweets" → automated repurposing workflow

### Pattern 4: Quality Checks
**Signal**: Notes describe reviewing or auditing things repeatedly
**Example**: Notes on "checking if my prompts follow best practices" → prompt linter

### Pattern 5: Research Pipelines
**Signal**: Notes describe a consistent research process
**Example**: Notes on "how I validate a business idea" → automated market research agent

## Trajectory Forecasting Logic

To forecast 3–6 months out, apply this logic:

1. **Identify the strongest cluster** — this becomes the user's primary expertise signal
2. **Find the fastest-growing cluster** — this is where to invest
3. **Identify the key gap** — the missing piece that unlocks the next level
4. **Project the combination** — what becomes possible when gap is filled?
5. **Find analogous creators** — who's 6 months ahead on this path? What did they ship?

### Growth Archetypes

**The Deepener**: Strong in one area, adding depth
→ Forecast: Becomes a go-to resource in that specific niche

**The Connector**: Medium in several areas, finding bridges
→ Forecast: Creates unique cross-domain products or frameworks

**The Builder**: Technical depth + business awareness
→ Forecast: Ships tools/SaaS at the intersection of expertise

**The Teacher**: Deep knowledge + communication notes
→ Forecast: Course, community, or newsletter business

Identify which archetype fits the vault and adjust forecast accordingly.
